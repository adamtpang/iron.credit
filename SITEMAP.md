# Iron sitemap and content strategy: stealing from Rain and Ramp

Two different playbooks to copy. **Rain** shows the information architecture of a serious payments-infrastructure company (clean Product and Solutions split, heavy social proof, trust and compliance pages). **Ramp** shows the programmatic SEO and content engine that made it the fastest-growing fintech in America (thousands of templated pages capturing every relevant search).

---

## What to steal from Rain (the structure)

Rain's site is organized as:
- **Product** by capability: card-issuing, payments, wallets, onramps, offramps, virtual-accounts, corporate-cards, rewards.
- **Solutions** by use-case and audience: global-card-programs, spend-stablecoins-like-cash, cross-border-b2b, instant-spendable-remittances, branded-wallet-in-a-box, global-access-to-us-dollars.
- **Resources**: a deep library of case studies ("how X launched in weeks"), partnership announcements (credibility), and "why we raised" posts.
- **Trust and compliance**: dedicated pages for SOC 2, PCI-DSS, "how Rain reduces risk," "how we keep your smart contracts safe."
- About, Press, Blog, Careers, Media kit, Legal, Contact. Multi-language (/es).

Lessons for Iron:
1. Split **Product** (what it is) from **Solutions or Who it is for** (bitcoiners, the rejected, by country).
2. Social proof is a content category, not a footnote: case studies and partnership announcements as their own URLs.
3. **Trust gets dedicated pages** (proof of reserves, security, non-custodial explainer, the graveyard). For a credit product this is the conversion engine, not a nicety.
4. Publish "why we raised" and milestone posts (build in public, the Adcock and Tesla move).

## What to steal from Ramp (the growth engine)

Ramp runs thousands of templated, programmatic pages, each targeting a specific search. The page-type templates:
- **/blog/[finance-term]** and how-to guides: an encyclopedia of finance ("what is X," "how to Y").
- **/vendors/[x]** and **/[x]-vs-[y]/alternatives**: comparison pages for every tool.
- **/versus/[competitor]**: head-to-head competitor pages (Brex, Amex, Divvy).
- **/charge-finder/[merchant]**: "what is this charge on my statement."
- **/expense-category/[category]** and **/[tool]-calculator/[state]**: free tools and utilities, templated by geography.
- **/integrations/[tool]**, **/rewards/[partner]**, **/customers/[name]**.
- **/leading-indicators**: the Ramp Economics Lab, thought leadership from their own proprietary spend data.
- **/authors/[name]**: author pages for search credibility.
- Free tools: invoice maker, expense policy builder, mission statement generator (link bait and lead gen).

The lesson: **one good template times hundreds of entries equals a moat of organic traffic.** Pick the templates that fit a bitcoin credit card.

## Iron's proposed information architecture

**Core pages (the product)**
- `/` home
- `/how` how it works (live)
- `/pricing` (live)
- `/card` the card itself, design and specs
- `/security` non-custodial, no rehypothecation, proof of reserves, the survivor design
- `/about` the story, founder, master plan
- `/manifesto` or `/plan` the public Iron Master Plan (the Tesla move)
- `/investors` (live), `/careers`, `/press`, `/blog`
- `/legal`, `/privacy`, `/terms`

**Who it is for (the barbell, Rain-style Solutions)**
- `/bitcoiners` for holders who refuse to sell
- `/rejected` for the people banks turned away
- `/[country]` Philippines first, then Indonesia, Vietnam

**Trust (the conversion engine)**
- `/graveyard` the hub: why crypto lenders and cards failed, and why Iron cannot
- `/graveyard/[company]` one page each: celsius, blockfi, voyager, ftx, genesis, cred (captures "what happened to X" and "is X safe" searches)
- `/proof-of-reserves` live attestation
- `/security` how your bitcoin is protected

**Learn (the moral, educational brand, Ramp's blog engine)**
- `/learn/[concept]`: what is a bitcoin-backed loan, what is LTV, what is liquidation, what is collaborative custody, what is rehypothecation, bitcoin loan vs selling, the cost of selling your bitcoin, how the economic machine works, what is a credit score

**Compare (Ramp's versus engine)**
- `/vs/[competitor]`: iron-vs-selling-your-bitcoin, iron-vs-nexo, iron-vs-ledn, iron-vs-coinbase-loans, iron-vs-strike, iron-vs-a-heloc, iron-vs-chase

**Tools (Ramp's calculators, link bait and lead gen)**
- `/calculator` how much can I borrow against my bitcoin
- `/ltv-calculator` health and liquidation simulator
- `/hodl-vs-sell` the tax and upside cost of selling versus borrowing

**Data (Ramp's leading-indicators, later)**
- `/index` the Iron Index: proprietary data on how bitcoiners borrow and repay, once we have it. The underwriting moat becomes thought leadership.

## Build order (by leverage, not by tidiness)

1. **`/graveyard` hub + `/security` + `/proof-of-reserves`.** Highest trust leverage and unique to us. The graveyard doubles as SEO (high-intent "is X safe" searches) and as the design-workshop story. Content already drafted in GRAVEYARD.md.
2. **`/calculator` (borrow-against-bitcoin).** One free tool that is shareable, captures intent, and feeds the waitlist. The single best lead-gen asset.
3. **`/vs/iron-vs-selling-your-bitcoin` and 2 or 3 competitor compares.** Captures people already deciding.
4. **`/learn` seed set (8 to 10 concepts).** The educational brand, and durable SEO. Start with the terms in the LTV and liquidation and custody cluster.
5. **`/[country]` Philippines page.** "bitcoin credit card philippines."
6. **`/manifesto`** the public master plan, and the `/bitcoiners` and `/rejected` audience pages.

Each of these is a template. Build the template once, then fill entries over time. That is how Ramp turned a website into a growth engine, and it is how Iron compounds attention while we build the product.
