# Iron Cofounder Playbook

How to find a partner you trust completely on both integrity and ability, no matter what you build together. Consolidates the founding-teams research with a search plan. Strategy lives in `MASTERPLAN.md`; this is the team layer.

## The bar: what "a second me" actually means

You do not want a clone. A clone is redundant, and the data says complementary beats identical. You want **the same caliber and the same character, with a different superpower**. Same soul, different skill. Someone you are as impressed by as you would want them to be of you.

The scorecard (a candidate must clear all seven):
1. **Integrity / full trust.** Would you hand them your keys, your money, and your reputation unsupervised? The All Blacks "no dickheads" filter: one selfish mindset infects the whole team. Non-negotiable, regardless of talent.
2. **Top-decile ability.** They are clearly excellent at their craft, and you learn from them. Smarter than you on their axis.
3. **Grit / resilience.** Evidence they finish hard things and recover from failure. SEAL selection data: resilience predicts who passes, raw talent does not. They will not ring the bell when it gets brutal.
4. **Complementary superpower.** They cover your gap. You are an engineer-CEO (vision, product, bitcoin, post-quantum). You most likely need a risk/credit or compliance/licensing complement, or a pure build-machine who frees you to be CEO. Not a smaller copy of you.
5. **Shared values + 25-year horizon.** The mission grips them, and they can say yes to building for a decade, not for a flip.
6. **The keeper test (Netflix).** If they were leaving for a similar role elsewhere, would you fight to keep them? If not, they are not it.
7. **Fun / energy.** You actually want them in the foxhole at 3am.

Trust usually comes from 3+ years of shared history. You cannot wait 3 years, so you **manufacture the crucible** (an intense project together) to compress that signal. More below.

## How the greats built teams (five transferable laws)

1. **Filter on character and fit, not just talent.** All Blacks ("better people make better All Blacks," no dickheads), SEAL selection (grit over physique), Barca (recruit for the system, not the physique).
2. **Keep talent dense and complementary, never redundant.** Bell Labs (a "critical mass" of different-shaped brilliant people in close proximity), Netflix ("talented people make one another more effective; the denser the talent, the less process you need"), the Bulls (one superstar plus deliberately complementary specialists: Pippen, Rodman the rebounder, not five scorers).
3. **Make the standard explicit and enforce it.** Jordan set a non-negotiable performance floor. The All Blacks enforce humility by ritual ("sweep the sheds," "champions do extra"). The standard is lived, not written in a deck.
4. **Build cohesion around a shared DNA, not vibes.** Barca's "way of playing" ran from the under-8s to the first team, so "every player knew the system already." Bell Labs mixed disciplines on purpose. Write down your operating model and values, then hire only people who fit it.
5. **Engineer the scene, then grant freedom.** Brian Eno's "scenius": genius is usually communal, produced by a fertile scene with mutual appreciation, fast tool-sharing, and shared credit. Bell Labs gave "circumscribed freedom" under technically literate leadership. The environment produces the output as much as the individuals.

## How to find them: the scenius strategy

The biggest mistake is hunting individuals in a small local pool. The greats did the opposite: they **inserted themselves into (or built) a scene that concentrated high-character, high-ability people**, and let proximity plus repeated collaboration do the matching. Terman's Stanford, the Fairchild spin-out, PayPal, Bell Labs all worked this way.

Five plays, in order:
1. **Mine your existing crucibles first.** 13 of 14 legendary teams knew each other before founding. Ex-collaborators, classmates, prior-project partners, the people you have already survived something with. (Resolve Christian: he is your most on-pattern lead and he is going cold.)
2. **Build the magnet.** A live product with traction recruits a peer (Instagram recruited its own power user; WhatsApp's Koum pulled in Acton). iron.credit/score plus building in public is the magnet. Great people join proof, not pitches.
3. **Insert yourself into concentrators at full size.** Not the 12 people on campus today, but the whole scene.
4. **Manufacture crucibles to compress trust.** You cannot wait years, so run intense shared projects: 1-day jams, a build weekend, a small hackathon. A hard project together manufactures the trust signal fast and is the truest interview (watch iteration velocity, grit, character under stress).
5. **Use formal matching as lead-gen.** YC cofounder matching (~25% of invites accept). Treat it as top-of-funnel, not a silver bullet.

## The AI-cofounder question (cofounder.co, summon.guide)

Honest answer: **leverage, not a substitute.** cofounder.co is an agentic company-OS (execution capacity across departments plus structure); it lets one operator run more surface area and need less process, exactly the Netflix density logic. summon.guide is AI mentorship with historical figures, a thinking and learning tool. Neither carries risk, holds equity, makes irreversible judgment under real ambiguity, brings an independent network, or is the second character who pushes back at 3am and keeps you honest. That function is inherently human (the no-dickheads, whanau, keeper-test layer).

The right use: lean on AI (cofounder.co, OpenClaw, Claude, summon for learning) to become a formidable solo founder, which does two things: it lets you move now without a cofounder, and it **raises the bar** for a human partner, because AI covers the execution gaps that used to force a rushed hire. AI buys you the patience to not settle. Do not skip the human; use AI to be choosier about them.

## What David Senra would say

- **Obsession is a magnet.** Become so obsessed and so clear on the mission that great people want in. The founder's job is to be worth joining.
- **Study how the greats did it** (which is this document) and **do not settle**, because a bad partner is worse than none.
